Curriculum

What You'll Learn

14 hours of intensive, hands-on learning across four modules. Click each module to explore the detailed curriculum.

1.1 Opening & Program Overview

30 min
  • Workshop objectives and expectations
  • Participant introductions and icebreaker activity
  • Overview of the Transformation Architect journey

1.2 Ikigai Mapping for Trainers

90 min
  • Introduction to the Ikigai framework
  • Individual reflection and mapping exercise
  • Partner/triad sharing and pattern identification
  • Identifying the intersection point for signature program focus

1.3 Strengths-Based Positioning

75 min
  • CliftonStrengths framework introduction
  • Strengths application exercise for training contexts
  • Skills inventory using Competency Self-Assessment Matrix
  • Identifying high-skill, high-enjoyment areas

1.4 Module Integration

25 min
  • Synthesis exercise connecting Ikigai and Strengths
  • Preliminary positioning statement draft
  • Q&A and Module 2 preview

2.1 Opening & Reconnection

15 min
  • Recap of Module 1 insights
  • Pre-work review (if applicable)
  • Module 2 objectives

2.2 Niche Selection & Market Analysis

90 min
  • Market demand analysis framework
  • Blue Ocean Strategy: Finding uncontested market space
  • Red Ocean vs Blue Ocean positioning exercise
  • Crafting a clear niche statement
  • Validation planning: 5 conversations methodology

2.3 Program Blueprint Development

90 min
  • Backward Design introduction (Wiggins & McTighe)
  • Identifying desired results and evidence of learning
  • Program Blueprint Canvas completion
  • Peer review and feedback in triads

2.4 Program Naming Workshop

25 min
  • Naming conventions and best practices
  • Program name brainstorming and evaluation
  • Finalizing working program name
  • Module 2 summary and closing

3.1 Opening & Recap

15 min
  • Session reconnection
  • Review of program blueprint and name
  • Module 3 objectives

3.2 4MAT Module Design

90 min
  • 4MAT Learning Cycle introduction
  • WHY: Motivation and meaning (Imaginative learners)
  • WHAT: Content and concepts (Analytic learners)
  • HOW: Practice and application (Common sense learners)
  • WHAT IF: Adaptation and creation (Dynamic learners)
  • Module design exercise and peer review

3.3 Session Outline Development

75 min
  • Full-day session outline creation
  • 90-20-8 Rule for energy management
  • Activity sequencing and timing
  • Visual framework creation for signature methodology

3.4 Module Integration

30 min
  • Rapid prototype preparation for teach-back
  • Session outline peer review
  • Module 4 preview and preparation instructions

4.1 Opening & Context

15 min
  • Review of completed program design
  • Module 4 objectives: From design to market
  • Teach-back preparation

4.2 Teach-Back Demonstrations

60 min
  • 10-minute micro-teaching demonstrations in triads
  • Structured peer feedback using evaluation criteria
  • Refinement notes and action items

4.3 Marketing Materials Creation

60 min
  • Marketing one-pager development
  • Program overview and unique value proposition
  • Learning outcomes and methodology highlights
  • Pitch deck outline (10-slide structure)

4.4 Pitch & Pricing Strategy

45 min
  • 60-second elevator pitch crafting
  • Pitch practice in pairs with feedback
  • Value-based vs. time-based pricing
  • Malaysian and international market benchmarks

4.5 Action Planning & Closing

30 min
