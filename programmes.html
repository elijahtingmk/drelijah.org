PRisMA 2024 · OSHA 2022

Supporting Your PRisMA 2024 & OSHA 2022 Compliance

Wellbeing   |   Risk Control   |   Capability Building

In line with the Occupational Safety and Health (Amendment) Act 2022, Malaysian employers now have a statutory “Duty of Care” to manage psychosocial risks just as they do physical ones. PRisMA 2024 is the official DOSH framework for meeting these obligations.

Where We Fit

Your PRisMA Journey

The PRisMA framework follows a three-stage process. Our programmes are specifically designed to support Stage III: Risk Control.

Stage I & II Screening & Assessment

Your internal Psychosocial Trained Person (PTP) or OHD identifies workplace hazards using tools like the LEO26 survey.

Our Speciality Stage III Risk Control Support

Once hazards are identified, the law requires a PRiMA (Psychosocial Risk Management Action Plan). This is where we step in.

Bridging the Gap

From “Assessment” to “Action”

Under the PRiMA Action Plan, employers must implement controls to mitigate identified hazards. Our programmes serve as Evidence-Based Administrative Controls for the most common workplace stressors.

High Work Demands

Mitigation through energy management and cognitive load training.

Low Job Control

Building adaptability and internal locus of control.

Poor Workplace Relationships

Enhancing EQ and assertive communication to prevent conflict.

Role Ambiguity

Developing strategic focus and proactive communication.

EPC23 Alignment

How Our Programmes Map to EPC23

Every programme on this page satisfies EPC23 items 16 & 20 — the Training & Education controls mandated in your PRiMA Action Plan. Individual programmes extend further into Social Support, Control, Workload, Job Matching, and Working Environment themes. The specific LEO26 components (JC / WD / JS), EPC23 items, and PRiMA themes each programme addresses are listed directly on every card below.

Scope note. Training-based controls address capability-building hazards. A complete PRiMA response may also require structural or policy controls — for example EPC23 items 2 (complaint-handling fairness), 9 (job security), 11 (staffing levels), 19 (employee voice), and 22 (workplace violence policy). These typically require HR policy work alongside training. We are happy to flag these in your scoping conversation.
