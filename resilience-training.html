Where This Sits

This is a distinct service from PRisMA OSH compliance. Resilience training is a proactive, preventative capability programme that an organisation can run at any time — it is not a statutory psychosocial-risk assessment and does not replace one. It is also non-clinical: it builds everyday skills and habits, and does not constitute clinical psychological therapy, psychiatric evaluation, or medical counselling.

The Evidence Base

The Predictive 6 Factor Resilience (PR6) Model

Developed by Hello Driven and used across organisations worldwide, PR6 maps resilience across six interconnected domains. Training and reinforcement are built around the domains where a team is most stretched — turning resilience from an abstract idea into specific, practisable behaviour.

Factor 01

Vision

Sense of purpose, goals, and meaning — the direction that keeps effort coherent when pressure rises.

Factor 02

Composure

Regulating emotions and staying calm under pressure — noticing reactivity early and steadying it.

Factor 03

Reasoning

Problem-solving, resourcefulness, and anticipation — thinking clearly when the path is uncertain.

Factor 04

Tenacity

Persistence, realistic optimism, and the capacity to recover and keep going after setbacks.

Factor 05

Collaboration

Support networks and connection — the relationships and communication that carry teams through strain.

Factor 06

Health

Sleep, nutrition, and physical wellbeing — the bodily foundation that everything else is built on.

Scope & Boundary

What This Training Is — and What It Is Not

Proactive, non-clinical capability-building. Workplace resilience training builds everyday skills, habits, and team behaviours. It is preventative and educational in nature, and does not constitute clinical assessment, psychological therapy, psychiatric evaluation, or medical counselling. Participants identified as needing clinical support are signposted to qualified providers.

Distinct from statutory PRisMA assessment. This training is not a substitute for the statutory LEO26 / EPC23 psychosocial-risk assessment cycle. Where an organisation carries OSHA 2022 obligations, those are handled separately under DOSH PTP competency on the PRisMA Compliance track.

Data handling under PDPA 2010. Any individual resilience data collected through assessment tools is processed under the Personal Data Protection Act 2010. Cohort reporting is de-identified; individual results are not disclosed to leadership without explicit consent.
